
`Our goal is not to get at the systems but to find all the ways to get there.`

> - What can we see?
> - What reasons can we have for seeing it?
> - What image does what we see create for us?
> - What do we gain from it?
> - How can we use it?
> - What can we not see?
> - What reasons can there be that we do not see?
> - What image results for us from what we do not see?

## 1. Enumeration Methodology

<img width="1652" height="794" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/21359f06-fd5e-42c2-9586-95fd28265f22" />

The whole enumeration process is divided into three different levels:
- `Infrastructure-based enumeration` (1,2)
- `Host-based enumeration` (3,4)
- `OS-based enumeration` (5,6)

|Layer	|Description	|Information Categories
|-|-|-|
|1. Internet |Presence	Identification of internet presence and externally accessible infrastructure.	|Domains, Subdomains, vHosts, ASN, Netblocks, IP Addresses, Cloud Instances, Security Measures|
|2. Gateway	|Identify the possible security measures to protect the company's external and internal infrastructure.	|Firewalls, DMZ, IPS/IDS, EDR, Proxies, NAC, Network Segmentation, VPN, Cloudflare|
|3. Accessible |Services	Identify accessible interfaces and services that are hosted externally or internally.	|Service Type, Functionality, Configuration, Port, Version, Interface|
|4. Processes	|Identify the internal processes, sources, and destinations associated with the services.	|PID, Processed Data, Tasks, Source, Destination|
|5. Privileges	|Identification of the internal permissions and privileges to the accessible services.	|Groups, Users, Permissions, Restrictions, Environment|
|6. OS Setup	|Identification of the internal components and systems setup.	|OS Type, Patch Level, Network config, OS Environment, Configuration files, sensitive private files|

### Layer No.1: Internet Presence

- This layer focuses on discovering testable targets within the contracted scope.
- We use various techniques to identify domains, subdomains, netblocks, and other infrastructure components that represent the company's Internet footprint.
- `The goal is to identify all possible target systems and interfaces for testing.`

### Layer No.2: Gateway
- We analyze reachable targets to understand their interfaces, protection mechanisms, and network positioning.
- `The goal is to understand what we're dealing with and potential obstacles.`

### Layer No.3: Accessible Services
- We examine each target's available services to understand their purpose, functionality, and implementation.
- Since administrators install services for specific reasons, we must learn how they work to interact with them effectively.
- `This layer aims to understand target system functionality and gain knowledge needed to communicate with and exploit systems effectively.`

### Layer No.4: Processes
- Every executed command or function processes data from user input or system generation, creating processes with specific tasks that have sources and targets.
- `The goal is to understand these factors and identify dependencies between them.`

### Layer No.5: Privileges
- Each service runs under specific users with defined permissions and privileges.
- These privileges often include functions that administrators overlook, particularly in Active Directory and multi-area administration environments.
- `It's crucial to identify these privileges and understand their capabilities and limitations.`

### Layer No.6: OS Setup
- We gather information about the operating system and configuration through internal access, providing insight into internal security and administrative team capabilities.
- `The goal is to assess administrative practices and identify sensitive internal information.`
